History

Syncronicity is a wonderful thing! So many things have came together to result in Hamilton becoming a City of Character.

In 2012, the Hamilton City Schools were recognized with the National School District of Character award, after many years of character education in our city schools.

Shortly after that, two students at Miami University Hamiltonâ€”Kofi Ansah and Shan Qkureshi--became concerned about a lack of civility on campuses, both locally and nationally, and helped create a highly developed, student-directed Project Civility program, under the guidance of Dr. Bob Rusbosin.

This led to the creation of a panel of Hamilton citizens--David Belew, Cindy Dingeldein, Derek Howard, Chrissy Hutzelman, Paul Thoms, Pat Moeller and Don Ryan--addressing the question of civility and character, at a Miami Hamilton Downtown forum titled Civility in the Community.

This inspired Mayor Pat Moehler to explore the possibility of becoming a City of Character. He then created the Hamilton City of Character Committee, comprised of community representatives, which officially launched their new program in April of 2014. This group has met regularly, since that time.

Paul Thoms served as chair of the committee; Steve Lippert currently holds that position.

Along the way we looked at programs that exist in several other cities across the nation, and, happily, also learned about the Character Council of Greater Cincinnati & Northern Kentucky, and that was a giant step.

And there you have it, syncronicity--people coming together for the good of the community as a whole. We hope you will become a part of that process.
